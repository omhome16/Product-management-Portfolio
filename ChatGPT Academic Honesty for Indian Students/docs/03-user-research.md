# 03. User Research: Qualitative Interview Findings

This document summarizes the qualitative insights gathered from interviewing 10 engineering students and 4 university educators, illustrating the structural learning gap and behavior dynamics.

---

## Student Cohort Findings (n = 10)

We identified three primary student personas based on their AI usage patterns:

### 1. The "Journal Copier" (Lab Report Automation)
* **Demographics:** 4 students (2nd and 3rd year).
* **Behavior:** Pasts raw lab manual instructions into ChatGPT to get a completed, formatted write-up, complete with conclusions and theory.
* **Comprehension Gap:** High. Students could not explain the core experimental concepts during oral examinations.
* **Direct Quote [YOUR INPUT NEEDED - Student Interview Details]:**
  > *"Our lab journals are graded on completeness and neatness. ChatGPT gives me the exact definitions, theory, and procedure in 5 seconds. I write it down word-for-word. I don't read what I'm writing, I just want my submissions done before the 5 PM deadline."* — 2nd-year Mechanical Engineering Student, Pune.

### 2. The "Struggling Coder" (Programming Tasks)
* **Demographics:** 4 students (2nd and 3rd year Computer Science/IT).
* **Behavior:** Uses ChatGPT to debug compiler errors or write complete data structure implementations (e.g., AVL tree rotation, Dijkstra's algorithm).
* **Comprehension Gap:** Medium-High. They can run the code, but they cannot explain why a pointer assignment is structured in a particular way or calculate the time complexity.
* **Direct Quote [YOUR INPUT NEEDED - Student Interview Details]:**
  > *"When my code throws a segmentation fault, I don't try to debug it anymore. I just paste the entire file into ChatGPT and ask 'fix this.' It gives me a clean, working version. It's great, but if someone asks me to trace the pointers on a blackboard during a class viva, I have no idea what's happening."* — 3rd-year CS Student, Mumbai.

### 3. The "Exam Crammer" (Concept Summarization)
* **Demographics:** 2 students (Final/4th year).
* **Behavior:** Feeds entire textbook chapters or syllabus documents into ChatGPT to get summary notes and potential exam questions.
* **Comprehension Gap:** Medium. They understand the surface definitions but lack deep analytical capacity for non-standard questions.
* **Direct Quote [YOUR INPUT NEEDED - Student Interview Details]:**
  > *"I use it to study the night before semester exams. I ask ChatGPT to explain concepts like 'deadlock detection' in 3 simple bullet points. It helps me pass the multiple-choice or short-answer questions, but I struggle on long-answer questions that ask us to design a solution."* — 4th-year ECE Student, Delhi.

## Educator Cohort Findings (n = 4)

We interviewed 4 educators to assess the impact of AI on grading and class performance.

### 1. The "Frustrated Lab Evaluator"
* **Role:** Professor & Department Head (Computer Engineering).
* **Observation:** Written submissions are flawless and identical, but oral vivas reveal a severe decline in fundamental coding skills.
* **Direct Quote [YOUR INPUT NEEDED - Educator Interview Details]:**
  > *"Ten years ago, students struggled with pointer errors, but by debugging them, they learned memory architecture. Today, their code compiles perfectly on day one because they copied it from ChatGPT, but when I ask them to explain a simple loop structure during class vivas, they struggle. I need a tool that forces them to think."* — Department Head, Computer Science, Tier-2 College.

### 2. The "Assessment Redesigner"
* **Role:** Professor (Physics/Electrical Engineering).
* **Observation:** Traditional take-home assignments have lost their evaluation utility.
* **Direct Quote [YOUR INPUT NEEDED - Educator Interview Details]:**
  > *"I have completely stopped grading homework sheets because they all look identical. I am forced to shift weight to surprise quizzes and pen-and-paper writing during class. If ChatGPT had an official 'educator-certified' mode that acted like a Socratic guide, I would integrate it into my curriculum."* — Associate Professor, Department of Physics, Tier-1 College.
