# 02. Research Plan: Understanding the Academic AI Loop

To design a solution that balances student productivity with genuine learning, I structured a research plan to investigate the qualitative behavior patterns of Indian engineering students and the core concerns of their educators.

---

## User Interview Design & Target Cohorts

The primary research phase target sample:
1. **Student Cohort (Cohort A):** 10–12 engineering students (2nd, 3rd, and 4th/final year) across Tier-1 (IITs, NITs) and Tier-2 (large private university-affiliated engineering colleges) institutions.
2. **Educator Cohort (Cohort B):** 4–5 professors or teaching assistants (TAs) who actively grade lab journals, programming assignments, and conduct viva-voce examinations.

---

## Student Cohort Interview Script (Cohort A)

These questions seek to understand actual workflows, student awareness of cheating, and potential resistance to a Socratic tutoring feature.

1. **Contextual Usage:**
   * What engineering subjects or tasks do you use ChatGPT for most frequently (e.g., writing MATLAB scripts, debugging C++ pointers, writing lab record introductions, explaining concepts)?
2. **Comprehension vs. Task Completion:**
   * When you copy an answer from ChatGPT to submit for an assignment, how much of that topic do you feel you've actually understood?
   * If you had a pop quiz on that exact topic 24 hours later, how confident would you feel?
3. **Accuracy & Experience:**
   * Have you ever submitted code or an explanation from ChatGPT that was factually incorrect or hallucinated? What happened during grading?
4. **Pedagogical Friction (Feature Testing):**
   * If ChatGPT offered a default "Tutor Mode" that asked you to explain your current understanding before giving the final code or answer, would you use it, or would you immediately switch to Claude or Gemini to bypass that friction?
5. **Institutional Regulations:**
   * Has your college or university administration formally announced any rules or guidelines regarding generative AI tools in submissions?

---

## Educator Cohort Interview Script (Cohort B)

These questions seek to identify how grading systems are adapting and what educators consider a "responsible study assistant."

1. **AI Output Detection:**
   * Can you reliably tell the difference between a lab code or theory report generated by ChatGPT and one written independently by a student?
2. **Observational Changes:**
   * Have you noticed a drop in students' performance in face-to-face evaluations (oral vivas, lab examinations, written tests) since ChatGPT became popular?
3. **Tool Endorsement:**
   * What specific guardrails would a generative AI tool need to have for you to formally recommend it as an approved study aid in your course syllabus?
4. **Grading & Assessment Design:**
   * How are you changing your assignments or grading criteria to account for students using ChatGPT?

---

## Secondary Research Strategy

1. **Regulatory Tracking:** Monitor annual announcements and circulars from the **All India Council for Technical Education (AICTE)** and the **University Grants Commission (UGC)** regarding AI governance and digitization.
2. **Academic Literature Review:** Evaluate academic papers concerning the "Desirable Difficulty" framework in STEM instruction, specifically looking at interactive Socratic agents vs. direct answer keys.
3. **App Sentiment Analysis:** Review discussions on developer and student-centric forums (Reddit's r/EngineeringStudents, r/BTech, and Twitter/X) to capture unfiltered sentiment regarding ChatGPT usage patterns in Indian universities.
