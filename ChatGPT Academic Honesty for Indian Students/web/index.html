01 — Executive Summary

The Strategic Thesis

The Problem

ChatGPT is optimized for instant direct answers. In higher technical education, this frictionless output enables students to submit coding and analytical lab assignments without comprehension, creating regulatory ban risks in India.

The Approach

Implement an opt-in "Tutor Mode" settings layer that intercepts academic queries using dynamic classifiers, replacing immediate solutions with interactive, Socratic guidance and domain-scoped confidence indicators.

Projected Impact

Align ChatGPT with India's engineering regulatory mandate (AICTE), increase multi-turn learning engagement by 3.5x, and create B2B2C university licensing pipelines by presenting a learning-first ecosystem.

02 — Context & Problem

The Complete-vs-Comprehend Misalignment

ChatGPT is designed to deliver immediate utility. However, active learning requires effortful processing. When an engineering student pastes their lab instructions (e.g., "Implement a Dijkstra queue in C++") and immediately receives a perfect, compiler-ready script, the system bypasses their cognitive cycle. The student passes their homework verification but remains unable to debug or write code independently.

For OpenAI, this is not just an ethical question; it is a critical regulatory hurdle. In 2024, the All India Council for Technical Education (AICTE) designated 2025 as the Year of Artificial Intelligence. Colleges are now mandated to implement digital integrity programs. An AI assistant perceived as actively deskilling students risks campus network bans, cutting OpenAI off from the world's largest engineering education funnel.

03 — Research Findings

Discovery and Competitive Diagnostics

Student Behavior Cohorts (n = 10)

Qualitative insights from 2nd, 3rd, and final-year engineering students.

Research Observation

The "Journal Copier" Behavior: Students prioritize task completion over retention. Flawless copy-pasted labs are submitted, but conceptual recall is zero after 24 hours.

  • 90% adoption rate: Students consult ChatGPT daily for lab sheets.
  • Zero-effort debug: AI fixes segmentation faults instantly, preventing compile debugging learning.
  • Viva dread: Fear of in-person evaluations where they must explain code logic.

Qualitative Interview Excerpt

"For our computer network labs, I just paste the prompt request and copy-paste the whole code block into my notebook. I get full marks on my submissions. But last week, during the internal viva, the professor asked me to explain the TCP handshake and I blanked out. ChatGPT did the homework, not me."

— 3rd-year CS student, Pune

Educator Concerns & Demands (n = 4)

Feedback from department heads and lab instructors.

Educator Core Insight

Assessment Vulnerability: Traditional homework sets have lost grading validity. Professors want to endorse interactive AI tools but require Socratic protections first.

  • Grading disconnect: Flawless code submissions contrast with failing viva grades.
  • Classroom shifts: Moving evaluations to surprise physical writing sessions.
  • Academic alignment: Demand for interactive study companions rather than direct generators.

Qualitative Professor Feedback

"Students submit flawless lab manuals, but when asked to modify a single line of code in the lab, they cannot explain how a pointer works. If ChatGPT offered a mode that acts like an interactive tutor rather than an answer machine, I would make it a mandatory part of my class syllabus."

— CS Department Lead, Mumbai

04 — Frameworks

Feasibility, Desirability, Viability (DFV)

Desirability

High value for educators and concept-focused students. Cheating-focused users will experience friction. Opt-in setting toggle balances the core tension, retaining shortcut-seekers without forcing compliance.

Feasibility

High feasibility using lightweight model classifiers and persona prompts. Avoids expensive foundation fine-tuning. Latency is minimized by caching routing profiles.

Viability

Protects ChatGPT from national university bans. Establishes institutional sales tunnels (ChatGPT for Education) through certified compliance with AICTE/UGC codes.
