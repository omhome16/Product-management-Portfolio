# Zomato Gold: Retention Beyond Discounts

> A Product Management case study exploring why Zomato Gold's discount-dependent value proposition creates structurally fragile retention — and how a non-discount "habit layer" can fix what deeper discounts never will.

**Author:** Om Nawale  
**Role Target:** Product Manager (AI/Growth)  
**Live Site:** [View on Vercel →](#)

---

## Problem Statement

Zomato Gold's 12.1 million subscribers generate >50% of all food delivery orders, but the subscription's entire value proposition is discount-based. When a subscriber's savings math tips unfavorable (fewer orders, rising platform fees, restaurants opting out), churn isn't a failure — it's rational. You can't fix rational churn with more discounts.

## Approach

Using JTBD, Kano Model, and RICE frameworks, this case study proposes a **non-discount retention layer** that addresses emotional and social jobs — personalized "Gold Picks," ML-driven churn prediction with reason-coded interventions, and food-identity features.
