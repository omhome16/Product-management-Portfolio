# Reflections: What I'd Do Differently

## Honest Assessment

### 1. Primary Research Gap

If I were doing this case study again with unlimited time, I'd run **8–10 actual subscriber interviews** rather than relying on public sentiment data. Reddit and Trustpilot skew negative — the users who are quietly satisfied with Gold don't post. That selection bias means my research likely overstates frustration and understates the functional value Gold genuinely provides to power users who order 15+ times a month.

The interview questions I'd ask are documented in [Research Plan — Primary Research section](./02-research-plan.md).

### 2. Supply-Side Economics

I'd push harder on the **restaurant partner side**. The Gold Pick concept sounds great from the subscriber angle, but the partner economics are the actual make-or-break:

- Who funds the "free dessert" — Zomato, the restaurant, or a cost-sharing model?
- What's the restaurant's incentive to participate beyond "more traffic"?
- Do restaurants see Gold as a fair program or an exploitative one?
- How does Zomato's commission structure interact with Gold perks?

A PM who proposes a feature without understanding the supply-side economics is proposing a fantasy, not a product.

### 3. Causal Validation

I'd want to **A/B test whether the "food identity" hypothesis actually holds**:

- Does a subscriber who engages with Gold Pick content really renew at higher rates?
- Or is engagement just a proxy for users who were already going to stay?
- Correlation isn't causation, and a good PM should be the first person to challenge their own thesis.

The proper test: randomly assign new Gold subscribers to a treatment group (with Gold Picks) and a control group (standard Gold), and measure M3 renewal rate difference. If the treatment group doesn't renew at statistically significantly higher rates, the hypothesis is wrong.

### 4. Regulatory Awareness

The CCPA's increasing scrutiny on food delivery platforms means that any retention strategy needs to be reviewed against dark pattern guidelines. The cancellation UX issues I documented aren't just user complaints — they're potential regulatory risk. A responsible PM should proactively audit the cancellation flow, not just the retention flow.

### 5. International Benchmarking

I focused almost entirely on the Indian food-tech market. Looking at how international subscription services (DoorDash DashPass, Uber One, Deliveroo Plus) handle retention beyond discounts would provide additional pattern recognition. Some of these programs have more mature retention playbooks that could inform Zomato's approach.

### 6. What I'm Most Uncertain About

The biggest unvalidated assumption is that **Zomato's brand can credibly own "food identity."** The company's recent focus has been on operational efficiency, margin improvement, and platform fees — not food content or curation. If the brand has moved too far toward "utility company" and away from "food company," the Gold Picks concept may feel inauthentic.

This would need to be tested with qualitative research: do subscribers perceive Zomato as a food discovery platform or just a delivery logistics tool?
