# Problem Statement

## Context

Zomato Gold is a subscription service (₹250–399/month) that offers free delivery on orders above ₹199 within a 7km radius, plus dining-out discounts at partner restaurants. As of March 2025, the program has **12.1 million active subscribers** who account for over 50% of Zomato's food delivery orders.

**Source:** [Eternal Ltd. FY25 Annual Report](https://www.eternal.com/investor-relations) — NSE India filings.

## The User

The user this case study focuses on is a Zomato Gold subscriber who:
- Joined 2–4 months ago, primarily for free delivery and restaurant discounts
- Orders 3–6 times per month
- Represents the segment most vulnerable to rational churn at Month 3

## The Specific Failure: Rational Churn

Zomato Gold's entire value proposition is discount-based. This creates a subscription dynamic that is **structurally fragile**:

1. **Subscribe** → "I'll save more than ₹299/month on delivery fees and discounts"
2. **Use** → Orders confirmed by free delivery + restaurant discounts
3. **Evaluate** → "Did I actually save more than I spent on the subscription?"
4. **Churn** → When the math tips unfavorable, cancellation is the rational choice

There is no emotional attachment, no identity formation, no behavioral habit formed around being a "Gold member." Churn is not a failure — it is rational. And you cannot fix rational churn with more discounts.

## Why Zomato Should Care

- **Subscription revenue predictability:** Subscription revenue is more predictable than per-order revenue — but only if churn is low
- **Subsidy economics:** The current model means Zomato is effectively paying for its own subscribers by subsidizing the discounts that retain them, without building durable loyalty
- **Competitive pressure:** Swiggy One bundles Instamart (grocery) into the subscription, making switch costs structurally higher without needing deeper discounts. Zomato needs a different defensible angle.

## Hypothesis

> If Zomato Gold adds a non-discount "habit layer" that addresses emotional and social Jobs-to-be-Done — personalized food discovery, identity-forming features, and ML-driven proactive retention — then M3 renewal rates will increase by 15–20% without requiring deeper discounts.

## Scope Boundaries

This case study focuses specifically on:
- **In scope:** Retention strategy for existing Gold subscribers, feature design, ML churn prediction, success metrics
- **Out of scope:** Pricing changes, Blinkit/Instamart integration, restaurant B2B partner acquisition, advertising strategy
