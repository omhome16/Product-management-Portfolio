## What This Data Tells Us

1. **Scale is not the problem.** 12.1M subscribers and >50% order share means Gold has achieved significant adoption. The challenge is retention quality, not acquisition volume.

2. **Revenue quality matters.** At an estimated ~10% revenue contribution, subscription revenue is meaningful but its primary value is in driving order frequency and reducing CAC — not direct margin.

3. **Margin improvement is real.** Food delivery EBITDA margin improving from 3.8% to 5.2% shows the underlying business is getting healthier. But this improvement is partly driven by platform fees that frustrate Gold subscribers.

4. **The growth vs. trust paradox.** +63.5% subscriber growth suggests strong acquisition, but this may mask high churn if the funnel top is large enough. Without public churn data, we can't assess retention health from subscriber count alone.
