# Research Plan

## Objective

Understand why Zomato Gold subscribers churn, what would make them stay beyond discounts, and how competitors solve this problem — using publicly available data sources.

## Research Methodology

### 1. Secondary Research: User Sentiment Analysis

Since primary interviews are not possible for this case study, I used public user feedback as proxy evidence. This introduces a known **selection bias** — users who post online skew negative, and quietly satisfied subscribers are underrepresented.

| Source | Method | What I Looked For |
|--------|--------|-------------------|
| Reddit (r/india, r/bangalore, r/mumbai) | Keyword search: "Zomato Gold", "not worth it", "cancel" | Churn triggers, value perception, comparison with Swiggy |
| Trustpilot | Zomato profile review analysis | Service quality complaints, Gold-specific frustrations |
| Google Play Store | Reviews mentioning "Gold" filtered by date | What delights and frustrates subscribers |
| Apple App Store | Reviews mentioning "Gold" membership | iOS-specific user sentiment patterns |
| Twitter/X | Search "Zomato Gold" filtered to last 12 months | Real-time reactions to policy changes (rain surcharge, etc.) |

## Research Limitations

1. **Selection bias in sentiment data:** Online reviews and Reddit posts skew negative. Users who find genuine value in Gold and order 15+ times/month are underrepresented in this dataset.
2. **No primary interviews:** I did not conduct direct subscriber interviews. Findings should be validated with 8–10 actual current/recent-ex Gold subscribers before making product decisions.
3. **No internal Zomato data:** Churn rates, cohort retention curves, and per-subscriber economics are not publicly available. The analysis relies on disclosed metrics and industry estimates.
4. **Competitive data gaps:** Swiggy One subscriber counts are not publicly disclosed, making direct scale comparisons impossible.

## If I Had Access to Primary Research

I would interview 8–10 Zomato Gold current or recent ex-subscribers with these questions:

1. Why did you subscribe to Gold in the first place? Walk me through that decision.
2. After subscribing, did your ordering behavior change? Order more? Same?
3. Do you think of yourself differently as a "Gold member"? Or does it just feel like a coupon in your wallet?
4. If you churned: what was the trigger moment — a specific experience or just "didn't feel worth it anymore"?
5. How does your Gold experience compare to Swiggy One if you've used both?
6. What would make you feel that your Gold membership was about your relationship with food — not just your relationship with a bill?
