The New Creator Gap

How engagement-biased recommendation algorithms create a structural obscurity loop for small podcasts — and a product strategy to retain content supply.

01 — Executive Summary

The Strategic Thesis

The Problem

Spotify's recommendation algorithm relies on historical engagement (completion rate, saves) to make predictions. Newly launched shows have zero signals, making them invisible, driving a 90% creator churn rate.

The Approach

Build a structured exploration framework consisting of a dedicated, niche-targeted "New Voices" home screen shelf for listeners and an actionable "Discovery Health" portal for creators to optimize metadata.

Projected Impact

Increase the Month 3 (M3) creator retention rate by 15–20% and stabilize catalog diversity. This secures Spotify's long-term defenses against search-first platforms like YouTube.

02 — Context & Problem

The Cold-Start Feedback Loop

Spotify's podcast acquisition strategy (Anchor, Megaphone) successfully captured supply creation tools. However, the discovery engine presents a fundamental barrier. By optimizing for high-confidence metrics like completion rate, the machine learning system creates a "rich get richer" dynamic.

A new show has no baseline interactions, meaning it receives no algorithmic distribution. With zero surfaces, it accumulates no data, sentencing the creator to permanent obscurity. This causes rapid creator abandonment, degrading catalog diversity, which directly harms listener retention for specialized niche content.

04 — Research Findings

Primary & Secondary Diagnostics

Cohort A: Small Creators

Sample Size: 8 Podcasters (<1 year, <500 listeners)

Research Case Profile

"Modern Carnatic Ragas Explained" (Chennai): Show age: 4 months. Averages 15 unique listeners per episode. 100% of traffic comes from direct messaging groups. Zero platform-driven discovery.

  • Analytics Obsession: Most check metrics multiple times daily in Month 1.
  • Promotion Exhaustion: Organic growth outside personal social circles is zero.
  • Algorithmic Obscurity: None of the 8 reported organic recommendations.

The Analytics Void

"I spend 10 to 12 hours editing each episode, adjusting audio levels. I put it on Spotify, and I get 15 plays. Unless I manually post the link directly to my WhatsApp student groups, the stats stay at absolute zero. It feels like the algorithm requires you to already be famous to be shown."

Cohort B: Niche Listeners

Sample Size: 6 active listeners seeking specialized topics

Research Case Profile

Niche Listener Log (Deep-Dive Tech Interest): Discovery path for last 3 shows: 2 from newsletter links (TLDR, Hacker News), 1 from guest cross-promotion. Zero from Spotify home recommendations.

  • Music vs. Podcast Gap: High trust in music algorithms, low trust in podcast recommendations.
  • Search Monopolies: Search listings are dominated by top-tier networks.
  • Manual Discovery: Find shows via newsletters, word-of-mouth, or third-party links.

The Recommendation Disconnect

"When I search for 'quantum computing,' Spotify's search returns corporate tech shows that discussed AI for 5 minutes. The real indie shows that dedicate hours to it are completely buried because they don't have thousands of reviews. It's highly frustrating."

05 — Frameworks

The CIRCLES Framework

C

Comprehend Situation

Focus on a two-sided marketplace. Solve the creator supply problem to protect long-term listener catalog engagement.

I

Identify Customer

Primary: New indie creator (<6 months, <500 listeners). Secondary: Specialized interest listener.

R

Report Needs

Creators require distribution signals and clear, transparent feedback. Listeners need high-relevance niche recommendations.

C

Cut Priorities

Target the cold-start structural block. Focus resources on programmatic exploration rather than manual editorial curation.

L

List Solutions

"New Voices" Home Shelf, Creator Optimization Dashboard, Algorithmic cold-start boost queue, Editorial form.

E

Evaluate Options

RICE prioritized. Shelf on home + dashboard score dashboard selected as top paired features.
