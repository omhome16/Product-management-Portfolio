# Chapter 3: Primary User Research & Sentiment

To ensure the product design is rooted in real-world behaviors, this chapter outlines the qualitative research findings from our primary target cohorts.

---

## Cohort A: Creator Interviews

### Creator Cohort Profile

* **Total Interviewed:** 8 Creators
* **Average Show Age:** 4.2 months
* **Average Listener Count (per episode):** 14 unique listeners
* **Primary Hosting Platform:** Spotify for Creators (formerly Anchor)

### Key Synthesis Patterns
1. **The "Analytics Void":** Creators check analytics multiple times daily in the first 30 days but experience frustration due to flatlines and a complete lack of attribution metrics (e.g., they cannot see *how* their 12 listeners arrived at the show).
2. **Promotion Exhaustion:** Growth is driven entirely by manual promotion (sharing with family, posting in Reddit forums, LinkedIn or Instagram stories). Once direct outreach stops, new listener acquisition drops to absolute zero.
3. **Algorithmic Disconnect:** Zero out of the 8 interviewed creators reported seeing any organic, algorithmically-driven listener acquisition on Spotify in their first 90 days.

### Structured Interview Records

#### Creator 1 (Niche: Classical Arts & History)
* **Show Details:** "Modern Carnatic Ragas Explained" — an independent music history and appreciation show launched by a Chennai-based classical vocalist.
* **Active Duration:** 4 months (8 episodes published).
* **Growth Profile:** Flat curve. Averages 15 unique listeners per episode, with 100% of traffic coming from direct shares.
* **Key Direct Quote:** 
  > *"I spend 10 to 12 hours editing each episode, adjusting audio levels, and researching histories. I put it on Spotify, and I get 15 plays. Unless I manually post the link directly to my WhatsApp student groups, the stats stay at absolute zero. It feels like the algorithm requires you to already be famous to be shown."*

#### Creator 2 (Niche: Local Startup Ecosystem)
* **Show Details:** "The Tier-2 Founder" — stories of bootstrapped businesses in Jaipur and Indore.
* **Active Duration:** 5 months (10 episodes published).
* **Growth Profile:** Flat growth. Reaches ~22 unique listeners per episode.
* **Key Direct Quote:**
  > *"My Spotify dashboard says '0% of your audience found you through Spotify Recommendations.' 100% is from my personal LinkedIn postings. I am starting to burn out because writing promotional copy on LinkedIn is taking more time than actually planning and recording my shows."*

#### Creator 3 (Niche: Climate Tech & Sustainability)
* **Show Details:** "Decarbonizing India" — weekly deep-dives into climate technology adoption.
* **Active Duration:** 3 months (6 episodes published).
* **Growth Profile:** Slow decay. Started with 35 listeners (friends), dropped to 8 active listeners.
* **Key Direct Quote:**
  > *"I've published 6 episodes. The analytics are a complete black box. I can't see search impressions or why my episode titles like 'Jaipur solar manufacturing' don't rank when people search for regional solar info. There's no feedback on how to make my show discoverable."*

#### Creators 4–8 Summary:
* **Cohorts Covered:** "Tech in Telugu" (regional tech updates), "The Indian Climate Investor" (venture capital focus), "Folk Instruments of India" (niche ethnomusicology), "Indie Author Blueprint" (writing advice), "Mental Health in College" (student journal).
* **Key Findings:** All creators expressed high enthusiasm at launch but described a rapid decline in motivation around episode 5. The primary driver of burnout was the "obscurity ceiling"—the realization that without pre-existing marketing budgets, organic search on Spotify fails to surface new uploads.

## Cohort B: Listener Interviews

### Listener Cohort Profile

* **Total Interviewed:** 6 Listeners
* **Average Weekly Consumption:** 6.5 hours
* **Discovery Preferences:** High preference for niche, narrative, or deep-dive educational podcasts.

### Key Synthesis Patterns
1. **Music vs. Podcast Recommendation Gap:** Listeners express high satisfaction with Spotify's music recommendation algorithms (e.g., Discover Weekly) but report that podcast recommendations are repetitive and dominated by massive, mainstream media networks.
2. **Search Fatigue:** When searching for specialized topics, the search results page prioritizes shows by total review volume and network backing, making it difficult to locate raw, independent, or highly focused content.

### Structured Interview Records

#### Listener 1 (Preference: Deep-Dive Tech & Engineering)
* **Discovery Log (Last 3 Shows):** 2 shows via technical newsletter recommendations (e.g., TLDR), 1 show from a guest appearance on an established podcast. Zero from Spotify's recommendations.
* **Spotify Recommendation Trust Rating:** 2/10.
* **Key Direct Quote:**
  > *"When I search for 'quantum computing,' Spotify's search returns corporate tech shows that discussed AI for 5 minutes. The real indie shows that dedicate hours to it are completely buried because they don't have thousands of reviews. It's highly frustrating."*

#### Listener 2 (Preference: Specialized Arts & Cultural History)
* **Discovery Log (Last 3 Shows):** 1 from a Twitter/X recommendation list, 2 from a custom subreddit recommendation thread (r/podcasts).
* **Spotify Recommendation Trust Rating:** 3/10.
* **Key Direct Quote:**
  > *"Spotify's homepage recommends the same 10 true-crime and celebrity interview shows over and over. I trust its music recommendations 10/10, but the podcast recommendations feel like a billboard for major networks. I have to go to Reddit to find interesting niche shows."*

#### Listeners 3–6 Summary:
* **Preferences Covered:** Sustainable agriculture, regional language audiobooks, history of astronomy, indie gaming reviews.
* **Key Findings:** Listeners actively seek niche audio content but describe Spotify’s search as "navigation-based" rather than "discovery-based." They report that unless they already know the name of a small show, they cannot locate it on the app. All expressed willingness to listen to "New Creator" shows if they were surfaced in a dedicated, category-relevant home shelf.
